Instant Pot Naan Bread

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon active dry yeast
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 3/4 cup warm water
  • 2 tablespoons plain yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons melted ghee or butter
  • Garlic (optional, for garlic naan), finely chopped
  • Fresh cilantro leaves, chopped (optional, for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Activate the yeast: In a small bowl, combine warm water, sugar, and yeast. Allow it to sit for 5-10 minutes until frothy.
  2. Prepare the dough: In a mixing bowl, sift together the flour, salt, and baking powder. Make a well in the center and add the activated yeast mixture, yogurt, and melted ghee/butter. Mix everything until it forms a soft dough. Knead the dough on a floured surface for about 5-7 minutes until smooth and elastic.
  3. Rise the dough: Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover it with a damp cloth, and let it rest in a warm place for 1-2 hours, or until it doubles in size.
  4. Preheat the Instant Pot: Turn on the Instant Pot and select the "Yogurt" setting. Allow it to preheat for a couple of minutes and then turn it off.
  5. Divide and shape the dough: Once risen, punch down the dough and divide it into 6-8 equal portions. Roll each portion into a ball and flatten slightly with your hands.
  6. Cooking the naan: Lightly flour the working surface. Roll out each dough portion into an oval or round shape, about 1/4 inch thick. Optionally, sprinkle chopped garlic onto the rolled-out dough and press it in. Brush the Instant Pot's inner pot with a little ghee or oil. Place one rolled-out naan into the preheated Instant Pot. Close the lid and let it cook for about 2 minutes.
  7. Finishing touches: After 2 minutes, carefully open the lid. The naan should have puffed up and have some brown spots. Using tongs, carefully remove the naan from the Instant Pot and brush the top with a little more melted ghee or butter. Optionally, sprinkle chopped cilantro leaves over the naan for added flavor.
  8. Repeat for remaining dough: Repeat the process with the remaining dough portions.
  9. Serve warm: Serve the naan warm with your favorite curries, dips, or as a side to any meal.
